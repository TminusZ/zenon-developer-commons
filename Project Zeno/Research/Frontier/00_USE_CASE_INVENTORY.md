# Project Zeno: Frontier Use-Case Inventory

**Document purpose:** Classification and inventory pass only. No Frontier documents are written here. This table supports the organizational decision of what maps into each existing `Project Zeno/Research/Frontier/` file, what belongs in the main `Research/` folder, and what should not be documented yet.

**Source of truth:** `SPEC.md` v1.3.0 governs on all conflicts. Every row is grounded in a spec section, a research doc, or an explicitly labeled reserved/deferred field.

**Stance:** Anti-hype. Reserved means reserved. Speculative means speculative. Nothing is called trustless that SPEC.md §1 does not make trustless. The Phase 1 profile specifies exactly one domain (WASM, `L1_NATIVE`, `SINGLE`, executor set of size 1).

## A. Global Disclaimer and Category Key

> **"Phase 1 specified" (P1S) means specified by SPEC.md for the Phase 1 profile. It does not mean the functionality is live, deployed, implemented, or available on mainnet.**

**Category codes:**

| Code | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **P1S** | Phase 1 specified: the capability is in the Phase 1 SPEC.md profile. Does not mean live, deployed, or implemented. |
| **RAP** | Reserved architecture path: schema or field is present; Core MUST reject activation under the Phase 1 profile. |
| **D23** | Deferred Phase 2/3 path: explicitly named in spec as Phase 2 or Phase 3. |
| **SIS** | Speculative infrastructure/service role: no spec assignment; ecosystem reasoning only. |
| **UDC** | Unsafe / do-not-claim: directly contradicted by spec or README. |

**Standing rules for this document:**

- Do not describe Phase 1 as trustless execution.
- Do not imply `L1_RELAYED` or `EXTERNAL_OBSERVED` are activatable in Phase 1.
- Do not imply EVM compatibility gives Ethereum asset, liquidity, security, or finality interoperability.
- Do not imply Bitcoin SPV solves BTC custody.
- Custody is separate from event verification.
- Proof-shaped data is different from observed data.
- DA serving is different from DA enforcement.
- Watcher replay is different from fraud-proof enforcement.
- Sentinel architectural fit is different from Sentinel spec assignment.
- QSR collateral thesis is different from QSR spec assignment.
- Permissionless relay mitigates liveness censorship if at least one honest relayer submits the data. Do not say it "defeats" withholding.
- Phase 2 reduces executor trust by adding fraud-proof enforcement, assuming DA availability and at least one honest challenger/watcher within the challenge window. Do not say it simply "removes trust."
- Sentinel/libp2p DA serving is architecturally named as a best-effort Phase 1 support role, but not a paid, economic, or protocol-enforced Sentinel market.
- For EXTERNAL_OBSERVED: the domain can verify replay consistency, not external truth.

> **Column note on "Can the Domain Verify It?":** "Yes" means deterministically replay-reproducible from committed inputs and the DA bundle, bounded by executor honesty under bonded attestation in Phase 1, unless the cell explicitly states on-chain Settlement verification (e.g. conservation invariant, outbox inclusion proof). Phase 1 does not include on-chain execution-correctness verification (SPEC §1, §2.1).
